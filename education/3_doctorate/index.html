Doctorate @ UC San Diego

Ph.D. in Computer Science

In 2019, I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science (Computer Engineering) at UC San Diego.

I was a computer security researcher in the Systems & Networking (Sysnet) and Security & Cryptography (Cryptosec) research groups, as well as a member of the Center for Evidence-based Security Research (CESR), the Center for Networked Systems (CNS), and the Aviation Cyber Security Group (Aerosec).

My research with CESR focused on understanding the ecosystem of manually-operated malware. My thesis, entitled Understanding the Remote Access Trojan malware ecosystem through the lens of the infamous DarkComet RAT, includes studies of remote access trojan (RAT) operators in high-fidelity honeypots and the deployment of sinkholes and scanners in order to detect and monitor command-and-control servers as well as infections.

My research with Aerosec focused on the development of the Triton testbed for avionics security research. We also examined vulnerabilities in consumer IoT devices meant to replicate electronic flight bags.
